Hi, I'm Seiji

I am a PhD student at the Nara Institute of Science and Technology (NAIST), working on clinical natural language processing. My research focuses on building practical and reliable language technologies for real-world health care settings, especially in Japanese clinical text.

A central theme of my work is robustness under domain shift. Across my publications, I study how models trained on one institution or document type can transfer to new hospitals and unseen note styles, including disease name recognition and broader clinical information extraction.

I also work on privacy-preserving and data-sharing-aware methods: source-free domain adaptation, LLM-assisted annotation when source data cannot be shared, and synthetic corpus generation (such as RecordTwin) to improve accessibility of clinical NLP research while maintaining safety constraints.

More recently, my publications include benchmark and evaluation efforts for practical clinical tasks in Japanese, as well as annotation methodologies that reduce expert burden (for example, QA-based temporal relation annotation). Overall, I aim to make clinical NLP systems both deployable and trustworthy in practice.
